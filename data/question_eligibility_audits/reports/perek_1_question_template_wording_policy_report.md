# Perek 1 Question Template Wording Policy Report

## Why This Policy Exists

The Bereishis Perek 1 eligibility audit identified 133 future input candidates that Yossi approved for planning only. This policy defines what later wording may safely ask and what it may not ask before any protected-preview planning can be considered.

## Deferred Families

- `basic_verb_form_recognition`: 25 rows remain deferred pending a separate morphology-question wording policy.

## What Future Questions May Ask

Future reviewed templates may ask about one clean reviewed token or function at a time, using the approved family boundaries. Vocabulary and noun recognition must stay basic and literal. Direct-object-marker wording must ask about the function of `את`, not a simple translation. Shoresh prompts must use clean reviewed root candidates.

## What Future Questions May Not Ask

Future templates may not ask unresolved verb-form questions, advanced stem/binyan questions, unreviewed translations, broad phrase translations from token-level candidates, theological interpretation, protected-preview content, or anything requiring answer choices or answer keys before the later review gate.

## Review Requirements Before Protected Preview

Before any protected-preview planning task, Yossi/teacher review must approve family policy, exact input rows, wording boundaries, answer type, answer-key language, distractor constraints, and context-display requirements.

## Safety Gates

- No questions generated.
- No answer choices generated.
- No answer keys generated.
- No protected-preview input list created.
- No protected-preview content created.
- No reviewed-bank entries created.
- No runtime changes made.
- No student-facing use approved.
