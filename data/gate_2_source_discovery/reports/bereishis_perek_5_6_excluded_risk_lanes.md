# Bereishis Perek 5-6 Excluded Risk Lanes

This report records lanes intentionally excluded from the Perek 5-6 review-only safe candidate inventory. Exclusion does not mean the source material is wrong; it means the lane is not safe for this source-discovery layer.

| Lane | Why excluded now | Future possibility |
|---|---|---|
| genealogy/name-heavy targets | Perek 5 contains long name chains and age formulas; names are excluded unless tied to a clear beginner skill. | Source-only later if teacher identifies a narrow purpose. |
| advanced shoresh/morphology | Forms such as `וַיִּתְהַלֵּךְ`, `הִנְנִי`, and `וַתִּשָּׁחֵת` need explicit grammar support. | Teacher/source review plus validator support required. |
| ambiguous word forms | `אָדָם` can be person/name/common noun depending on context; `אֱלֹהִים` requires reverent/context care. | Teacher review required. |
| phrase_translation without whole-phrase review | Phrase-level examples exist but Perek 3 policy requires whole-phrase distractor review first. | Possible later source-only phrase lane. |
| context-heavy interpretation | Perek 6 moral/flood context can become comprehension rather than basic word skill. | Teacher-authored objective required. |
| repeated token clusters | `בָּנִים`, `בָנוֹת`, `שָׁנָה`, `בָשָׂר`, `תֵּבָה`, and genealogy formulas repeat heavily. | Spacing/session-balance review required. |
| translation uncertainty | No local teacher-reviewed translation review layer exists for Perek 5-6. | Translation review required. |
| weak source confidence beyond canonical text | No Perek 5-6 curriculum extraction or verified source-to-skill map was found. | Build/review provenance layer first. |
| non-beginner grammar | Construct phrases, plural forms, and ark-building technical terms may be too advanced without teaching context. | Teacher review required. |

## Perek 5 examples

- Genealogy/name-heavy targets: repeated names and age formulas across Bereishis 5.
- Repeated family vocabulary: `בָּנִים`, `בָנוֹת`, `בֵּן`, and repeated `שָׁנָה` forms need spacing review.
- Advanced verbs: `וַיִּקְרָא`, `וַיּוֹלֶד`, and `וַיָּמֹת` are not included as beginner source-discovery candidates.

## Perek 6 examples

- Context-heavy interpretation: `חָמָס`, `רָעַת`, and flood-context phrases require teacher framing.
- Ark-building vocabulary: `תֵּבַת`, `עֲצֵי־גֹפֶר`, `קִנִּים`, `צֹהַר`, and `פֶתַח` require review before any future packet planning.
- Phrase translation: `בְנֵי־הָאֱלֹהִים`, `בְּנֹת הָאָדָם`, and `אַנְשֵׁי הַשֵּׁם` are not included because phrase-level policy requires whole-phrase review.

## Safety status

All excluded lanes remain source-discovery only. They are not runtime, not reviewed-bank, not protected-preview, and not student-facing.
