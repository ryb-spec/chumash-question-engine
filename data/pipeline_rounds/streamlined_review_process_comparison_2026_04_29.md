# Streamlined Review Process Comparison - 2026-04-29

## Current/old process step count estimate

Recent Perek work often used 11 to 14 recurring steps once source discovery, teacher review, candidate planning, readiness reports, decision reports, prompt artifacts, internal packet creation, observation templates, and next-Perek gates were counted separately.

## Previous 8-phase streamlined model

An eight-phase version would still separate teacher review from candidate planning and keep more readiness-only gates.

## New 7-phase model

The new model has seven phases: Source Discovery Bundle; Combined Teacher Review + Candidate Planning Checklist; Combined Teacher/Planning Decisions Applied; Protected-Preview Candidate Review + Readiness; Protected-Preview Decisions Applied + Internal Packet Readiness; Internal Packet + Review Checklist; Observation Decisions + Next-Gate Authorization.

## Percent reduction estimate

Reducing 11 to 14 repeat steps to 7 phases is a reduction of roughly 36% to 50%.

The new standard reduces repeat pipeline phases by at least 35% by combining paperwork-only gates while preserving human decision and observation gates.

## What gets combined

Risk reports, duplicate/session-balance warnings, status indexes, teacher review, candidate planning, decision application, readiness summaries, prompt scopes, and review checklists are bundled when they do not represent new evidence or a new human decision.

## What remains separate

Source discovery, teacher review, candidate planning decision, protected-preview candidate review, internal packet review, observation evidence, and runtime promotion gate remain non-negotiable gates.

## Safety gates preserved

Runtime activation, active scope expansion, reviewed-bank promotion, protected-preview packet creation, and student-facing content stay false unless a later explicit task changes them.

## Risks of over-compression

Over-compression can hide source-confidence changes, obscure held items, or blur revision-watch status. The phase JSON contract and one phase validator mitigate that risk.

## Recommendation for next use

Use this process for the next future Perek batch and do not rewrite historical artifacts.
