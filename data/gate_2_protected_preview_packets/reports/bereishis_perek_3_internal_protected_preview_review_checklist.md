## Reviewer instructions

For each item, check:

- Hebrew token accuracy
- Hebrew phrase/context accuracy
- Target word is truly a simple noun
- Prompt clarity
- Answer choice fairness
- Expected answer correctness
- Explanation accuracy
- No accidental translation/context claim beyond noun recognition
- No confusing prefix/suffix/morphology issue
- No over-repetition or fatigue risk
- Appropriate level/difficulty
- Source/provenance confidence
- Whether item should remain internal only, be revised, need follow-up, or be rejected

## Decision options

These are blank reviewer fields only. Do not set these decisions in data from this checklist.

- [ ] `approve_for_limited_post_preview_iteration`
- [ ] `approve_with_revision`
- [ ] `needs_follow_up`
- [ ] `reject_for_broader_use`
- [ ] `source_only`

## Global review questions

- Are two עֵץ items too repetitive, or useful as controlled repeated noun-recognition practice?
- Does the pair of Bereishis 3:7 items, עֲלֵה and תְאֵנָה, create a clear noun-recognition contrast or unnecessary overlap?
- Are all four items measuring basic noun recognition and not translation/context?
- Are distractors plausible but not misleading?
- Are these items appropriate for the target student level?
- Should any item require teacher wording revision before further use?
