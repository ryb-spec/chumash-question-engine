# Bereishis Perek 1 Approved Input Candidate Wording Policy

## Status And Boundaries

This document is a policy only. It does not generate questions, answer choices, answer keys, protected-preview inputs, protected-preview content, reviewed-bank entries, runtime data, or student-facing content.

This policy does not approve protected preview. It does not approve reviewed bank. It does not approve runtime. It does not approve student-facing use. It only defines future safe wording boundaries for the 133 Bereishis Perek 1 input candidates already approved for later review planning.

Every later generated item still needs separate review. That later review must include template review, wording review, source review, and gate approval before any protected-preview generation can be considered.

## Inputs Covered

The policy covers these first-pass approved future input-candidate families:

- `vocabulary_meaning`
- `basic_noun_recognition`
- `direct_object_marker_recognition`
- `shoresh_identification`

The policy keeps this family deferred:

- `basic_verb_form_recognition`

## Global Wording Rules

Allowed future wording must remain token-level, literal, and teacher-reviewable. Context phrases may support student orientation, but the assessment target must remain the reviewed token or reviewed function.

Forbidden future wording includes generated answer choices, answer keys, interpretive or theological claims, broad phrase translation when the candidate is token-level, and any item that depends on unresolved morphology or unreviewed source evidence.

## Family Policy: `vocabulary_meaning`

Allowed future question intent:

- Ask for the basic meaning of a reviewed Hebrew token.
- Use one clean Hebrew token at a time.
- Use only the reviewed/source-supported meaning.
- Keep prompt wording simple and literal.
- Allow context phrase only as support, not as the object of assessment.

Allowed future wording shape, as non-final template shells only:

- `What does [HEBREW_TOKEN] mean in this phrase?`
- `Choose the meaning of [HEBREW_TOKEN].`
- `In this pasuk phrase, [HEBREW_TOKEN] means...`

Forbidden:

- Do not ask about unreviewed translations.
- Do not ask for full phrase translation.
- Do not ask for interpretation or deeper meaning.
- Do not combine multiple vocabulary tokens in one item.
- Do not use context-dependent words without teacher wording review.
- Do not create distractors in this task.
- Do not generate answer choices in this task.

Required teacher review:

- Confirm Hebrew token.
- Confirm English meaning.
- Confirm whether context phrase is needed.
- Confirm no theological or conceptual sensitivity.
- Confirm distractors later come only from approved banks.

## Family Policy: `basic_noun_recognition`

Allowed future question intent:

- Ask whether a reviewed token is a noun, object, person, place, or thing category, depending on approved skill language.
- Ask students to identify a simple noun token.
- Use only token-level verified 3.01 rows.

Allowed future wording shape, as non-final template shells only:

- `Which word is a noun?`
- `What type of word is [HEBREW_TOKEN]?`
- `Which Hebrew word names a thing/person/place?`
- `Identify the noun in this short phrase.`

Forbidden:

- Do not use multi-lane phrases as the assessment target.
- Do not ask about adjectives, constructs, suffixes, or forms unless separately reviewed.
- Do not ask about grammar labels beyond the approved level.
- Do not ask students to distinguish advanced parts of speech.
- Do not treat every vocabulary word as automatically question-safe.
- Do not generate answer choices in this task.

Required teacher review:

- Confirm noun status.
- Confirm age and level appropriateness.
- Confirm the token is not construct/suffix-sensitive unless separately approved.
- Confirm wording does not overteach or overclaim.

## Family Policy: `direct_object_marker_recognition`

Allowed future question intent:

- Ask about the function of `את` as a direct-object marker.
- Ask students to recognize that `את` points to the object receiving the action.
- Use only clean token-level direct-object-marker rows.
- Require careful wording because `את` is not translated directly as a regular word.

Allowed future wording shape, as non-final template shells only:

- `What is the job of [ET_MARKER] in this phrase?`
- `Which word marks the direct object?`
- `In this phrase, [ET_MARKER] helps point to what is being acted upon.`

Forbidden:

- Do not ask "What does את mean?" as if it has a simple English translation.
- Do not translate `את` as "the."
- Do not translate `את` as "with."
- Do not use unclear phrases where `את` may confuse students.
- Do not combine `את` with conjunction or prefix analysis unless separately approved.
- Do not generate answer choices in this task.

Required teacher review:

- Confirm the phrase has clean direct-object-marker usage.
- Confirm wording explains function, not translation.
- Confirm distractors do not teach wrong meaning.
- Confirm the item is appropriate for the intended level.

## Family Policy: `shoresh_identification`

Allowed future question intent:

- Ask for the shoresh/root of a reviewed Hebrew token.
- Use only clean, token-level verified root candidates.
- Prefer tokens where the shoresh is visible enough for students.
- Require teacher confirmation before use.

Allowed future wording shape, as non-final template shells only:

- `What is the shoresh of [HEBREW_TOKEN]?`
- `Which shoresh is inside [HEBREW_TOKEN]?`
- `Identify the root letters of [HEBREW_TOKEN].`

Forbidden:

- Do not ask shoresh for unresolved or compound forms.
- Do not ask shoresh where prefixes/suffixes make the token too hard.
- Do not ask advanced binyan/stem questions.
- Do not ask students to infer a root from an unreviewed form.
- Do not generate answer choices in this task.

Required teacher review:

- Confirm root.
- Confirm which letters students should identify.
- Confirm whether prefixes/suffixes must be explained first.
- Confirm no ambiguity.

## Deferred Family Policy: `basic_verb_form_recognition`

Status: deferred.

No `basic_verb_form_recognition` candidate may become an input candidate until a separate morphology-question wording policy exists.

No future protected-preview input list may include this family yet.

Future policy must address:

- tense labels
- vav-hahipuch
- future/jussive ambiguity
- imperative forms
- person/gender/number
- when to ask visible form vs. meaning
- how to avoid overclaiming

## Review Gate Before Protected Preview

Before any later protected-preview planning task, Yossi/teacher review must approve:

- the family-level wording boundary
- the exact source token or phrase used as input
- the intended answer type
- whether context display is required
- whether the item has sensitivity or ambiguity risks
- later distractor-bank constraints
- later answer-key wording

This policy is not itself permission to generate questions or protected-preview inputs.
